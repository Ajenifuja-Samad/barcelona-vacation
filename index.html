Picture Perfect Trip: Barcelona, Spain

A three-paragraph snapshot of places, activities, and food.

Where would you go?

I would travel to Barcelona, Spain, a coastal city where Gothic spires meet modernist curves. My must-see locations include iconic architecture and neighborhoods that reveal the city’s layered history and football culture. The energy around Plaça de Catalunya flows toward the sea, and the skyline is crowned by Gaudí’s unfinished masterpiece.

Top locations on my route

  • La Sagrada Família (Eixample)
  • Camp Nou / Spotify Camp Nou Tour (Les Corts)
  • Barri Gòtic (Gothic Quarter) and the Cathedral of Barcelona
  • Park Güell (Carmel Hill)

What would you do?

I would take a stadium tour and try to catch a match, stroll La Rambla at dusk, and ride the funicular up to Montjuïc for panoramic views. A day trip to Montserrat would add hiking and monastery time, and I’d end evenings with a seaside walk along Barceloneta. Between museum stops and neighborhood wandering, I’d build in time for street performances and local markets.

What would you eat?

I’d start with small plates—patatas bravas, croquetas, and pan con tomate—before sharing a seafood paella near the beach. For dessert, churros dipped in thick chocolate, and perhaps a creamy crema catalana. With meals, I’d try local cava or a glass of tinto de verano, keeping coffee breaks frequent and unhurried.
